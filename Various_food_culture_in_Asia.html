Central Asian cuisine


Central Asian nations share similarities in cuisine with neighboring West and East Asian nations, particularly Iran and Mongolia. Common meats include horsemeat and mutton, similar to beef in Mongolian cuisine. Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan have evolved their cuisine for nomad lifestyles. Central Asia is also known for yogurt production.
Ex: Horsemeat,Yogurt

East Asian cuisine


East Asian cuisine comprises Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Mongolian, Taiwanese, and Tibetan food, with staples like rice, noodles, seafood, mutton, bok choy, and tea, particularly in China.
Ex:Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Mongolian, Taiwanese, and Tibetan foods

North Asian cuisine


North Asian cuisine is often associated with Russian cuisine, but some regions in Siberia, such as the Yakuts and Yamal, have their own unique cuisine. Pelmeni, a Permic or Ugric dish, has become a popular dish in Russian cuisine. Key ingredients include fish and cowberries, and Yakuts traditionally enjoy kumis as a drink.
Ex: Yamal cuisine, Cowberries, Kumis, Pelmeni

South Asian cuisine


South Asian cuisine, originating from the Indian subcontinent, is rich in spices and combines multi-cuisine culture from around the world. Popular dishes include Poori Bhaji, Idli, Dosa, Dal Baati, and Litti Chokha. Vegetables are eaten with Chapati, rice is dal-soaked, and tea and coffee are popular. Common meats include lamb, goat, fish, and chicken. Beef is less common due to Hinduism's special place for cattle, and pork is considered taboo. Fish is common in South India, Sri Lanka, and the Bengal region. The northern region diet mainly consists of wheat and other crops.
Ex:India, Sri Lanka, and Bengal Foods

Southeast Asian cuisine


Southeast Asian cuisine emphasizes lightly prepared, aromatic dishes with citrus (such as lime, coriander/cilantro and basil) and herb flavors, substituting fish sauces for galangal, tamarind, and lemongrass. Cooking methods balance stir-frying, boiling, and steaming.
Ex:Kaffir lime, Basil, Galangal, Tamarind, Lemongrass & stir-frying(Cooking methods)

West Asian cuisine


West Asian cuisine is a diverse and homogeneous cuisine originating from various countries and peoples in the region. Common ingredients include olives, pitas, honey, sesame seeds, dates, sumac, chickpeas, mint, and parsley. Popular dishes include kibbeh and shawarma. The diet is based on cereals like wheat, rice, barley, and maize, with bread being a universal staple. Butter and clarified butter are traditionally used for cooking, while olive oil is used in Mediterranean coastal areas. Lamb and mutton are the favored meats, with grilled meats and vegetable stews being popular. Vegetables and pulses are the predominant staples, used in various ways.
Ex:Dates, Sumac, Chickpeas, parsley, Barley
Popular dishes: kibbeh and shawarma.
